1. Scale: The G‑Pulse Index ranges from 0 (extremely deflationary) to 100 (extremely inflationary). It measures global price pressures and inflation expectations using AI‑driven sentiment analysis of earnings calls, ISM reports, BLS CPI releases, shipping statements, inflation news, and commodity price movements.

2. What "inflationary/deflationary" means: The score reflects language about input costs, wage growth, pricing power, supply chain bottlenecks, and demand strength. A score above 60 suggests accelerating price pressures; below 40 suggests easing price pressures or weak demand.

3. Dual display: The headline shows live → 7d avg:
– Live score – the immediate average of today's document scores, reacting instantly to new data.
– 7d avg – a 7‑day moving average, designed to filter out noise and reveal the underlying trend.

4. Market expectation: A companion score derived from breakeven inflation rates (TIPS) and commodity prices (gold, oil), displayed below the GIP data. It is produced by a separate service and serves as a real‑world check against the document‑driven GIP score.

5. Confidence: HIGH, MEDIUM, or LOW based on the breadth of sources analysed and the total text volume.
